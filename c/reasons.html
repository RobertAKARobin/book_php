It isn't too basic.
This book probably isn't for you if you don't know how to click and drag the icon on your desktop.
It isn't too advanced.
This book probably isn't for you if you do know how to compare the semantic values of the tags <header>, <head>, <h1> and <h6>.
It's short.
You could realistically print this out at home.
It's efficient.
You don't need to memorize the dictionary to learn Spanish. You just need to know how the grammar works and how to use the dictionary.
It teaches you only what you need to know.
Being able to build a catalytic converter won't help you drive a car. Being able to build a webserver won't help you make an awesome website.
It tells you why.
There's a reason for everything in web design. Knowing those reasons makes it less scary.
You don't need a computer.
Most web design books expect you to try stuff on the computer as you read. I hate that. I always end up on Facebook. My goal is for you to read this end-to-end without having to touch a computer.

Read this aloud:

What does a cloud wear on its butt? Thunderpants.

Now try reading it this way:

What does a cloud wear on its butt?


Thunderpants.

It sounds funnier the second way. That's because the first part has a different purpose from the second part. Putting a space in there gives you a hint that thunderpants is the punchline.

A computer won't get the hint. Letters and spaces are just pieces of data in its memory. They don't mean anything. To tell a computer that joke, you'd need to say something like this:

Hey computer, this is a joke. Hey computer, this is the set-up. What does a cloud wear on its butt? Hey computer, that's the end of the set-up. Hey computer, this is the punchline. Thunderpants. Hey computer, that's the end of the punchline. Hey computer, that's the end of the joke.

Here's another way of writing that:

    <div class="punchline">Thunderpants.</div>
</div>

This is how webpages are written. It's called HTML.

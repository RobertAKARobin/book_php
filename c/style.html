I'm trying to figure out the costumes for Hamlet. I ask Bill to mark up the script with style suggestions.

When I get the script back, it's a hundred times longer. The content isn't any different — every time a character speaks a line, Bill writes what they're wearing. For example:

BERNARDO (Wearing armor and carrying a spear) Who's there?

FRANCISCO (Wearing armor and carrying a spear) Nay, answer me: stand, and unfold yourself.

BERNARDO (Wearing armor and carrying a spear) Long live the king!

I write back, Hey, Bill, think you could just write on a different piece of paper what the costumes should be for each type of person?

The next version of the script is back to normal length, with all the costume suggestions listed on a separate page: Each guard wears armor and carries a spear.

Styling is figuring out how you want elements to look. For example, you'd probably want a header to stand out from whatever it introduces, so you might just underline it, or you might make its font huge and orange and bold or something.

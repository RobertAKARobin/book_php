If you have a domain and a server you've got your phone and phone number. Now you need a reson for people to call you: you need to upload files to your website.

Loading is copying data from one place to another. Uploading is copying to the Internet; downloading is copying from the Internet. People always get this wrong. For example:

I'm going to download the photos of Little Timmy's birthday party to Facebook.

Ooh, Karen just put the photos of Little Timmy's birthday party on Facebook! I'll go upload them to my computer.

Most hosts let you upload stuff via a GUI, pronounced gooey. That's basically a webpage that lets you create, edit, and upload files through your web web browser, the same way you'd create, edit, or upload posts or pictures on Facebook.

GUI means Graphical User Interface. An interface is a program that makes it easier to work with some other program. A graphical interface uses graphics, probably because whatever you're working with is really hard to understand, like a server.

When you go to your local Chinese take-out restaurant the big menu above the cash register with photos of all the food is a GUI for people who don't speak Chinese. Your web browser is an interface for the Internet.

My hosting service uses a GUI called cPanel (as in "control panel"). There are others like Plesk and DirectAdmin that look different but do the same thing.

I actually usually use an FTP client called FileZilla to upload files to my site. It makes my site's host show up on my desktop so I can drag-and-drop files into it (like DropBox, if you've used that). It's quicker than cPanel but harder to set up.

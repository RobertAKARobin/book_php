You don't actually own a domain or a phone number, you sort-of rent it. You're the only one who can use it until your lease expires.

Renting a domain usually costs less than $20. You can rent as many as you want from a registrar, like IWantMyName.com or GoDaddy.com. Registrars all sell the same domains.

Domains are so cheap that it's really easy to hoard them. I'll come across some catchy word or phrase and think, I should totally make a website named that, and buy the domain. The result is I have a couple dozen domains collecting dust.

Phone numbers have area codes, like mine, 513 for southwestern Ohio. I haven't lived there in 7 years. Domains have top-level domains (TLDs) like .com, which is short for company. Lots of .coms have nothing to do with business.

A few TLDs are restricted, like .gov, which can only be used by governments. The rest don't have rules. I live in the USA but could buy a .ru domain (short for 'Russia') and write the whole thing in Chinese.

There used to be just a few TLDs. Now there's .in for India, .es for Spain, .attorney, .pink, .sexy, and many more. These might be cheaper than .com but will make your website's URL harder to remember. Many businesses choose their names by which .com domains are available.

You might think, I should buy a bunch of cheap domains, wait for people to want to buy them from me, and sell them at a high price! That's called domain squatting. It's sleazy, not worth it, and sucks for everyone else.

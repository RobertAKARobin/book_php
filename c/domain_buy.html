Like a phone number, you don't actually own a domain. You sort-of rent it from a registrar, like IWantMyName.com or GoDaddy.com. Renting a domain usually costs less than $20, and you can have as many as you want.

Domains are so cheap that it's really easy to hoard them. I'll come across some catchy word or phrase and think, I should totally make a website named that, and buy the domain. The result is I have a couple dozen domains collecting dust.

Phone numbers have area codes. Mine is513 for southwestern Ohio even though I don't live there. Domains have top-level domains (TLDs). My website TLD is .com for company even though I'm not a company.

A few TLDs are restricted, like .gov which can only be used by governments. The rest don't really have rules. I live in the USA but could buy a .ru domain (short for 'Russia') and write the whole thing in Chinese.

There used to be a small number of TLDs. Now there's .in for India, .es for Spain, .attorney, .pink, .sexy, and many more. .com is one of the oldest and most familiar to people, so it's the most popular.

That's one reason so many new companies have weird names. If you run a business selling cat photos it would make sense to name your business CatPhotos. But if catphotos.com is taken, and catphotos.net and whiskerly.com are available, you're probably better off naming your business Whiskerly.

You might see websites that are totally blank except for a message saying, Buy this domain for $2000! That's called domain squatting: people buying domains just to jack up the price and re-sell them, not to actually use them. It's sleazy, really annoying, and might be illegal soon.
